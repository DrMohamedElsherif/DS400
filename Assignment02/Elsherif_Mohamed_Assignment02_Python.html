# get the ratings of TV series and movies
query_ratings_distribution = """
SELECT t.titleType, r.averageRating
FROM titles t
JOIN ratings r ON t.tconst = r.tconst
WHERE t.titleType IN ('movie', 'tvSeries');
"""
# Execute query
ratings_data = pd.read_sql(query_ratings_distribution, conn)

# histogram for movie and TV series ratings
sns.set(style="whitegrid")
plt.figure(figsize=(10, 5))
sns.histplot(data=ratings_data, x="averageRating", hue="titleType", multiple="stack", bins=20, kde=False, palette="Set1")
plt.title("Distribution of Ratings for Movies vs. TV Series", fontsize=14)
plt.xlabel("Average Rating", fontsize=12)
plt.ylabel("Frequency", fontsize=12)
plt.grid(False)
plt.show()
TV Series are generally rated higher than movies, with more frequent ratings above 7 and fewer very low ratings. On the other hand Movies have a wider distribution of ratings, from extremely low to very high. There also seem to be extreme outliers on both ends for both movies and TV series. Some titles have ratings as low as 1.9 (TV series), and others as high as 9.2 (movies), indicating that both genres have extremes in viewer reception.
